
## THE INFERNO

1. **THE FOREST** — _Midway upon the journey of our life / I found myself within a forest dark, / For the straightforward pathway had been lost (Inf. I, 1–3)._
2. **THE PANTHER** — _And lo! almost where the ascent began, / A panther light and swift exceedingly, / Which with a spotted skin was covered o'er! (Inf. I, 31–33)._
3. **THE LION** — _He seemed as if against me he were coming / With head uplifted, and with ravenous hunger (Inf. I, 46, 47)._
4. **THE SHE-WOLF** — _"Behold the beast, for which I have turned back; / Do thou protect me from her, famous Sage" (Inf. I, 88, 89)._
5. **VIRGIL AND DANTE** — _Then he moved on, and I behind him followed (Inf. I, 136)._
6. **VIRGIL AND DANTE** — _Day was departing (Inf. II, 1)._
7. **BEATRICE AND VIRGIL** — _"Beatrice am I, who do bid thee go" (Inf. II, 70)._
8. **THE GATE OF HELL** — _"All hope abandon, ye who enter in!" (Inf. III, 9)._
9. **CHARON AND THE RIVER ACHERON** — _And lo! towards us coming in a boat / An old man, hoary with the hair of eld, / Crying: "Woe unto you, ye souls depraved!" (Inf. III, 82–84)._
10. **THE EMBARKATION OF THE SOULS** — _Charon the demon, with the eyes of glede, / Beckoning to them, collects them all together, / Beats with his oar whoever lags behind (Inf. III, 109–111)._
11. **LIMBO—THE INNOCENT SOULS** — _"Lost are we, and are only so far punished, / That without hope we live on in desire" (Inf. IV, 41, 42)._
12. **LIMBO—POETS AND HEROES** — _Thus I beheld assemble the fair school / Of that lord of the song pre-eminent, / Who o'er the others like an eagle soars (Inf. IV, 94–96)._
13. **MINOS** — _There standeth Minos horribly, and snarls; / Examines the transgressions at the entrance; / Judges, and sends according as he girds him (Inf. V, 4–6)._
14. **THE LUSTFUL** — _The infernal hurricane that never rests / Hurtles the spirits onward in its rapine (Inf. V, 31, 32)._
15. **PAOLO AND FRANCESCA** — _"O Poet, willingly / Speak would I to those two, who go together, / And seem upon the wind to be so light" (Inf. V, 73–75)._
16. **PAOLO AND FRANCESCA** — _"Love has conducted us unto one death; / Caïna waiteth him who quenched our life!" (Inf. V, 106, 107)._
17. **PAOLO AND FRANCESCA** — _"That day no farther did we read therein" (Inf. V, 138)._
18. **PAOLO AND FRANCESCA** — _I swooned away as if I had been dying, / And fell, even as a dead body falls (Inf. V, 141, 142)._
19. **CERBERUS** — _And my Conductor, with his spans extended, / Took of the earth, and with his fists well filled, / He threw it into those rapacious gullets (Inf. VI, 25–27)._
20. **THE GLUTTONS—CIACCO** — _"For the pernicious sin of gluttony / I, as thou seest, am battered by this rain" (Inf. VI, 53, 54)._
21. **PLUTO AND VIRGIL** — _"Be silent thou accursed wolf; / Consume within thyself with thine own rage" (Inf. VII, 8, 9)._
22. **THE AVARICIOUS AND PRODIGAL** — _"For all the gold that is beneath the moon, / Or ever has been, of these weary souls / Could never make a single one repose" (Inf. VII, 64–66)._
23. **THE STYX—THE IRASCIBLE** — _"Son, thou now beholdest / The souls of those whom anger overcame" (Inf. VII, 115, 116)._
24. **THE STYX—PHLEGYAS** — _The antique prow goes on its way, dividing / More of the water than't is wont with others (Inf. VIII, 29, 30)._
25. **THE STYX—PHILIPPO ARGENTI** — _Then stretched he both his hands unto the boat; / Whereat my wary Master thrust him back (Inf. VIII, 40, 41)._
26. **THE PORTALS OF DIS** — _I could not hear what he proposed to them; / But with them there he did not linger long, / Ere each within in rivalry ran back (Inf. VIII, 112–114)._
27. **THE ERINNYS** — _"This is Megaera, on the left-hand side; / She who is weeping on the right, Alecto; / Tisiphone is between" (Inf. IX, 46–48)._
28. **THE ANGEL** — _He reached the gate, and with a little rod / He opened it, for there was no resistance (Inf. IX, 89, 90)._
29. **BURNING GRAVES—THE HERESIARCHS** — _"My Master, what are all those people / Who, having sepulture within those tombs, / Make themselves audible by doleful sighs?" (Inf. IX, 124–126)._
30. **FARINATA** — _As soon as I was at the foot of his tomb, / Somewhat he eyed me, and, as if disdainful, / Then asked of me, "Who were thine ancestors?" (Inf. X, 40–42)._
31. **THE TOMB OF ANASTASIUS** — _We drew ourselves aside behind the cover / Of a great tomb, whereon I saw a writing, / Which said: "Pope Anastasius I hold" (Inf. XI, 6–8)._
32. **THE MINOTAUR** — _And on the border of the broken chasm / The infamy of Crete was stretched along, / Who was conceived in the fictitious cow (Inf. XII, 11–13)._
33. **THE CENTAURS—NESSUS** — _Beholding us descend, each one stood still, / And from the squadron three detached themselves, / With bows and arrows in advance selected (Inf. XII, 58–60)._
34. **CHIRON** — _Chiron an arrow took, and with the notch / Backward upon his jaws he put his beard (Inf. XII, 77, 78)._
35. **THE HARPIES' WOOD** — _They make laments upon the wondrous trees (Inf. XIII, 15)._
36. **THE SUICIDES** — _Then stretched I forth my hand a little forward, / And plucked a branchlet off from a great thorn; / And the trunk cried, "Why dost thou mangle me?" (Inf. XIII, 31–33)._
37. **THE SUICIDES** — _And two behold! upon our left-hand side, / Naked and scratched, fleeing so furiously, / That of the forest every fan they broke (Inf. XIII, 115–117)._
38. **THE BLASPHEMERS—CAPANEUS** — _O'er all the sand-waste, with a gradual fall, / Raining down dilated flakes of fire (Inf. XIV, 28, 29)._
39. **BRUNETTO LATINI** — _"Are you here, Ser Brunetto?" (Inf. XV, 30)._
40. **GERYON—SYMBOL OF DECEIT** — _And that uncleanly image of deceit / Came up and thrust ashore its head and bust (Inf. XVII, 7, 8)._
41. **THE DESCENT ON THE MONSTER** — _Onward he goeth, swimming slowly, slowly; / Wheels and descends (Inf. XVII, 115, 116)._
42. **DEVILS AND SEDUCERS** — _Ah me! how they did make them lift their legs / At the first blows! (Inf. XVIII, 37, 38)._
43. **PARAMOURS AND FLATTERERS** — _I saw a people smothered in a filth / That out of human privies seemed to flow (Inf. XVIII, 113, 114)._
44. **THAÏS** — _"Thaïs the harlot is it, who replied / Unto her paramour, when he said, 'Have I / Great gratitude from thee?'—'Nay, marvellous'" (Inf. XVIII, 133–135)._
45. **THE SIMONISTS** — _"O doleful soul, implanted like a stake," / To say began I, "if thou canst, speak out" (Inf. XIX, 47, 48)._
46. **DEVILS AND BARRATORS** — _They seized him then with more than a hundred rakes (Inf. XXI, 52)._
47. **DEVILS AND VIRGIL** — _But he cried out: "Be none of you malignant!" (Inf. XXI, 72)._
48. **BARRATORS—GIAMPOLO** — _Therefore he moved, and cried: "Thou art o'ertaken" (Inf. XXII, 126)._
49. **ALICHINO AND CALCABRINA** — _But sooth the other was a doughty sparhawk / To clapperclaw him well; and both of them / Fell in the middle of the boiling pond (Inf. XXII, 139–141)._
50. **TUMULT AND ESCAPE** — _Hardly the bed of the ravine below / His feet had reached, ere they had reached the hill / Right over us (Inf. XXIII, 52–54)._
51. **THE HYPOCRITES** — _They had on mantles with the hoods low down / Before their eyes (Inf. XXIII, 61, 62)._
52. **THE HYPOCRITES—CRUCIFIED PHARISEE** — _"This transfixed one, whom thou seest, / Counselled the Pharisees that it was meet / To put one man to torture for the people" (Inf. XXIII, 115–117)._
53. **THIEVES** — _Among this cruel and most dismal throng / People were running naked and affrighted (Inf. XXIV, 91, 92)._
54. **TRANSFORMATION INTO SNAKES** — _"O me, Agnello, how thou changest! / Behold, thou now art neither two nor one" (Inf. XXV, 68, 69)._
55. **EVIL COUNSELLORS** — _"Within the fires the spirits are; / Each swathes himself with that wherewith he burns" (Inf. XXVI, 47, 48)._
56. **SCHISMATICS—MAHOMET** — _He looked at me, and opened with his hands / His bosom, saying: "See now how I rend me" (Inf. XXVIII, 29, 30)._
57. **SOWER OF DISCORD** — _"Call to remembrance Pier da Medicina" (Inf. XXVIII, 73)._
58. **BERTRAM DE BORN** — _By the hair it held the head dissevered, / Hung from the hand in fashion of a lantern, / And that upon us gazed and said: "O me!" (Inf. XXVIII, 121–123)._
59. **GERI DEL BELLO** — _But said Virgilius: "What dost thou still gaze at? / Why is thy sight still riveted down there / Among the mournful, mutilated shades?" (Inf. XXIX, 4–6)._
60. **FORGERS** — _Such a stench came from it / As from putrescent limbs is wont to issue (Inf. XXIX, 50, 51)._
61. **FORGERS** — _Every one was plying fast the bite / Of nails upon himself, for the great rage / Of itching which no other succour had (Inf. XXIX, 79–81)._
62. **FORGERS** — _"That mad sprite is Gianni Schicchi, / And raving goes thus harrying other people" (Inf. XXX, 32, 33)._
63. **MYRRHA** — _"That is the ancient ghost / Of the nefarious Myrrha, who became / Beyond all rightful love her father's lover" (Inf. XXX, 37–39)._
64. **THE GIANTS—NIMROD** — _"Soul idiotic, / Keep to thy horn, and vent thyself with that, / When wrath or other passion touches thee" (Inf. XXXI, 70–72)._
65. **EPHIALTES** — _"This proud one wished to make experiment / Of his own power against the Supreme Jove" (Inf. XXXI, 91, 92)._
66. **ANTAEUS—DESCENT TO THE LAST CIRCLE** — _But lightly in the abyss, which swallows up / Judas with Lucifer, he put us down (Inf. XXXI, 142, 143)._
67. **COCYTUS—TRAITORS** — _"Look how thou steppest! / Take heed thou do not trample with thy feet / The heads of the tired, miserable brothers!" (Inf. XXXII, 19–21)._
68. **TRAITORS—BOCCA DEGLI ABATI** — _Then by the scalp behind I seized upon him, / And said: "It must needs be thou name thyself, / Or not a hair remain upon thee here" (Inf. XXXII, 97–99)._
69. **UGOLINO AND ARCHBISHOP RUGGIERI** — _The uppermost on the other set his teeth, / There where the brain is to the nape united (Inf. XXXII, 128, 129)._
70. **UGOLINO** — _"I calmed me then, not to make them more sad" (Inf. XXXIII, 64)._
71. **UGOLINO AND GADDO** — _"Gaddo / Threw himself down outstretched before my feet, / Saying, 'My father, why dost thou not help me?'" (Inf. XXXIII, 67–69)._
72. **UGOLINO** — _"Then hunger did what sorrow could not do" (Inf. XXXIII, 75)._
73. **THE JUDECCA—LUCIFER** — _"Behold Dis, and behold the place / Where thou with fortitude must arm thyself" (Inf. XXXIV, 20, 21)._
74. **THE WAY TO THE UPPER WORLD** — _The Guide and I into that hidden road / Now entered, to return to the bright world (Inf. XXXIV, 133, 134)._
75. **THE POETS EMERGE FROM HELL** — _Thence we came forth to rebehold the stars (Inf. XXXIV, 139)._

## THE PURGATORIO

76. **VENUS** — _The beauteous planet, that to love incites, / Was making all the orient to laugh, / Veiling the Fishes that were in her escort (Purg. I, 19–21)._
77. **CATO OF UTICA** — _I saw beside me an old man alone, / Worthy of so much reverence in his look, / That more owes not to father any son (Purg. I, 31–33)._
78. **THE VESSEL** — _But when he clearly recognized the pilot, / He cried: "Make haste, make haste to bow the knee! / Behold the Angel of God!" (Purg. II, 27–29)._
79. **THE CELESTIAL PILOT** — _Beatitude seemed written in his face (Purg. II, 44)._
80. **THE MOUNTAIN'S FOOT** — _On the left hand appeared to me a throng / Of souls, that moved their feet in our direction, / And did not seem to move (Purg. III, 58–60)._
81. **THE ASCENT** — _We mounted upward through the rifted rock, / And on each side the border pressed upon us (Purg. IV, 31, 32)._
82. **THE LATE REPENTERS** — _There were persons there / Who in the shadow stood behind the rock, / As one through indolence is wont to stand (Purg. IV, 103–105)._
83. **THE LATE REPENTERS** — _Meanwhile along the mountain-side across / Came people in advance of us a little, / Singing the Miserere verse by verse (Purg. V, 22–24)._
84. **BUONCONTE DA MONTEFELTRO** — _"God's Angel took me up, and he of hell / Shouted: 'O thou from heaven, why dost thou rob me?'" (Purg. V, 104, 105)._
85. **PIA** — _"Do thou remember me who am the Pia; / Siena made me, unmade me Maremma" (Purg. V, 133, 134)._
86. **SORDELLO AND VIRGIL** — _So he appeared; and then bowed down his brow, / And with humility returned towards him, / And, where inferiors embrace, embraced him (Purg. VII, 13–15)._
87. **THE DELL** — _"Salve Regina," on the green and flowers / There seated, singing, spirits I beheld (Purg. VII, 82, 83)._
88. **THE SERPENT** — _Hearing the air cleft by their verdant wings, / The serpent fled, and round the Angels wheeled (Purg. VIII, 106, 107)._
89. **TWILIGHT** — _The concubine of old Tithonus now / Gleamed white upon the eastern balcony (Purg. IX, 1, 2)._
90. **THE EAGLE** — _Terrible as the lightning he descended, / And snatched me upward even to the fire (Purg. IX, 29, 30)._
91. **THE PORTALS OF PURGATORY** — _I saw him seated on the highest stair, / Such in the face that I endured it not. / And in his hand he held a naked sword (Purg. IX, 80–82)._
92. **THE SCULPTURES** — _The wretched woman in the midst of these / Seemed to be saying: "Give me vengeance, Lord, / For my dead son" (Purg. X, 82–84)._
93. **THE PRIDEFUL—ODERISI** — _Abreast, like oxen going in a yoke, / I with that heavy-laden soul went on (Purg. XII, 1, 2)._
94. **ARACHNE** — _O mad Arachne! so I thee beheld / E'en then half spider (Purg. XII, 43, 44)._
95. **THE ENVIOUS** — _Covered with sackcloth vile they seemed to me, / And one sustained the other with his shoulder, / And all of them were by the bank sustained (Purg. XIII, 58–60)._
96. **SAPIA** — _"Sienese was I," it replied, "and with / The others here recleanse my guilty life" (Purg. XIII, 106, 107)._
97. **THE STONING OF STEPHEN** — _Then saw I people hot in fire of wrath, / With stones a young man slaying, clamorously / Still crying to each other, "Kill him! kill him!" (Purg. XV, 106–108)._
98. **MARCO THE LOMBARD** — _"Now who art thou, that cleavest through our smoke, / And art discoursing of us even as though / Thou didst by calends still divide the time?" (Purg. XVI, 25–27)._
99. **MARCO THE LOMBARD** — _"Thee will I follow far as is allowed me," / He answered; "and if smoke prevent our seeing, / Hearing shall keep us joined instead thereof" (Purg. XVI, 34–36)._
100. **THE SLOTHFUL** — _But taken from me was this drowsiness / Suddenly by a people, that behind / Our backs already had come round to us (Purg. XVIII, 88–90)._
101. **ASCENT TO THE FIFTH CIRCLE** — _"What aileth thee, that aye to earth thou gazest?" / To me my Guide began to say, we both / Somewhat beyond the Angel having mounted (Purg. XIX, 52–54)._
102. **THE AVARICIOUS—ADRIAN V** — _"What cause," he said, "has downward bent thee thus?" / And I to him: "For your own dignity, / Standing, my conscience stung me with remorse" (Purg. XIX, 130–132)._
103. **THE AVARICIOUS** — _Onward we went with footsteps slow and scarce, / And I attentive to the shades I heard / Piteously weeping and bemoaning them (Purg. XX, 16–18)._
104. **THE GLUTTONS—FORESE** — _"Ah, do not look at this dry leprosy," / Entreated he, "which doth my skin discolor, / Nor at default of flesh that I may have" (Purg. XXIII, 49–51)._
105. **THE GLUTTONS** — _And shadows, that appeared things doubly dead, / From out the sepulchres of their eyes betrayed / Wonder at me, aware that I was living (Purg. XXIV, 4–6)._
106. **THE TREE** — _People I saw beneath it lift their hands, / And cry I know not what towards the leaves (Purg. XXIV, 106, 107)._
107. **THE SEVENTH CIRCLE** — _There the embankment shoots forth flames of fire, / And upward doth the cornice breathe a blast / That drives them back, and from itself sequesters (Purg. XXV, 112–114)._
108. **THE SEVENTH CIRCLE** — _"Summae Deus clementiae," in the bosom / Of the great burning chanted then I heard, / Which made me no less eager to turn round (Purg. XXV, 121–123)._
109. **THE SEVENTH CIRCLE—THE LUSTFUL** — _And spirits saw I walking through the flame; / Wherefore I looked, to my own steps and theirs / Apportioning my sight from time to time (Purg. XXV, 124–126)._
110. **LEAH** — _Youthful and beautiful in dreams methought / I saw a lady walking in a meadow, / Gathering flowers (Purg. XXVII, 97–99)._
111. **TERRESTRIAL PARADISE** — _Already my slow steps had carried me / Into the ancient wood so far, that I / Could not perceive where I had entered it (Purg. XXVIII, 22–24)._
112. **APOCALYPTIC PROCESSION** — _The four and twenty Elders, two by two, / Came on incoronate with flower-de-luce (Purg. XXIX, 83, 84)._
113. **CHARITY, HOPE AND FAITH** — _One so very red / That in the fire she hardly had been noted. / The second was as if her flesh and bones / Had all been fashioned out of emerald; / The third appeared as snow but newly fallen (Purg. XXIX, 122–126)._
114. **BEATRICE** — _Appeared a lady under a green mantle, / Vested in color of the living flame (Purg. XXX, 32, 33)._
115. **SUBMERSION IN LETHE** — _The beautiful lady opened wide her arms, / Embraced my head, and plunged me underneath, / Where I was forced to swallow the water (Purg. XXXI, 100–102)._
116. **THE WHORE AND THE GIANT** — _Upright beside her I beheld a giant; / And ever and anon they kissed each other (Purg. XXXII, 152, 153)._
117. **THE EUNOË** — _If, Reader, I possessed a longer space / For writing it, I yet would sing in part / Of the sweet draught that ne'er would satiate me (Purg. XXXIII, 136–138)._

## THE PARADISO

118. **THE MOON** — _Saw I many faces prompt to speak (Par. III, 16)._
119. **MERCURY** — _So I beheld more than a thousand splendors / Drawing towards us (Par. V, 103, 104)._
120. **VENUS—CHARLES MARTEL** — _"That left-hand margin, which did bathe itself / In Rhone, when it is mingled with the Sorgue, / Me for its lord awaited in due time" (Par. VIII, 58–60)._
121. **THE SUN—GLORIFIED SOULS** — _In such wise of those sempiternal roses / The garlands twain encompassed us about, / And thus the outer to the inner answered (Par. XII, 19–21)._
122. **MARS** — _Well was I ware that I was more uplifted / By the enkindled smiling of the star, / That seemed to me more ruddy than its wont (Par. XIV, 85–87)._
123. **THE CROSS** — _Here doth my memory overcome my genius; / For on that cross as levin gleamed forth Christ, / So that I cannot find ensample worthy (Par. XIV, 103–105)._
124. **CACCIAGUIDA** — _"You are my ancestor, / You give to me all hardihood to speak, / You lift me so that I am more than I" (Par. XVI, 16–18)._
125. **JUPITER** — _The holy creatures / Sang flying to and fro (Par. XVIII, 76, 77)._
126. **JUPITER** — _O soldiery of heaven, whom I contemplate, / Implore for those who are upon the earth / All gone astray after the bad example! (Par. XVIII, 124–126)._
127. **THE EAGLE** — _Appeared before me with its wings outspread / The beautiful image that in sweet fruition / Made jubilant the interwoven souls (Par. XIX, 1–3)._
128. **THE EAGLE** — _Those living luminaries all, / By far more luminous, did songs begin / Lapsing and falling from my memory (Par. XX, 10–12)._
129. **BEATRICE** — _Already on my Lady's face mine eyes / Again were fastened (Par. XXI, 1, 2)._
130. **SATURN** — _A stairway I beheld to such height / Uplifted, that mine eye pursued it not (Par. XXI, 29, 30)._
131. **THE HEAVEN OF THE FIXED STARS** — _"Begin then, and declare to what thy soul / Is aimed, and count it for a certainty, / Sight is in thee bewildered and not dead" (Par. XXVI, 7–9)._
132. **THE HEAVEN OF THE FIXED STARS** — _"Glory be to the Father, to the Son, / And Holy Ghost!" all Paradise began, / So that the melody inebriate made me (Par. XXVII, 1–3)._
133. **CRYSTALLINE HEAVEN** — _Not otherwise does iron scintillate / When molten, than those circles scintillated (Par. XXVIII, 89, 90)._
134. **THE EMPYREAN** — _In fashion then as of a snow-white rose / Displayed itself to me the saintly host (Par. XXXI, 1, 2)._
135. **THE QUEEN OF HEAVEN** — _"Thou shalt behold enthroned the Queen / To whom this realm is subject and devoted" (Par. XXXI, 116, 117).
